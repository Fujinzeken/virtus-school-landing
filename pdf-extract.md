EDUCATIONAL PROCESS AND EDUCATIONAL STANDARDS
Lesson Schedule
Lesson schedules at Milestone International School are designed to keep students focused and concentrated, making their learning process effective and interesting.
Lessons in elementary grades are short and focused, divided into two parts of 40 minutes each. This method is optimal for focusing students' attention on one topic. After the main topic is explained in the first lesson, in-depth analysis and practical exercises are conducted in the second lesson.
Classes for middle and upper classes provide a wider and deeper learning process. Lessons are divided into 40-minute parts; interactive methods are used to increase effectiveness.
Schedule for Pupils Living in the Dormitory:

06:00 — Wake up
07:00–08:00 — Self-study
08:00 — Breakfast
08:35–16:05 — Lessons (40-minute parts, with 5-minute breaks)
13:10–13:40 — Lunch
16:30–18:30 — Additional classes and clubs
19:00–21:00 — Self-study

Structure of Lessons and Breaks
Lessons are divided into 80-minute segments, with two 40-minute parts within each segment. Short breaks help to renew students' attention.
Psychological and Physiological Benefits
The lesson and break schedule is designed taking into account the physiological and psychological needs of students. Short and effective lessons are organized for young children, and a deep and comprehensive educational process is organized for adults. Light and well-ventilated classrooms help students to concentrate and feel comfortable.

INTERNATIONAL BACCALAUREATE (IB) PROGRAM
Milestone International School offers the International Baccalaureate (IB) system in accordance with international education standards. This program greatly contributes to the intellectual, personal, emotional and social development of students.
What is the IB Program?
IB is an internationally recognized educational system that teaches students independent, critical thinking, creative and scientific approach. This education will prepare your child for the best universities around the world.
Advantages of the IB Program:

Provides high-quality theoretical and practical knowledge
The IB Diploma gives access to the most prestigious universities
Develops independent thinking, analysis and creative approach
Comprehensive curriculum covers various subjects for all-round development
Broadens students' worldview and increases their sense of social responsibility

IB Programs:

Primary Years Programme (PYP) (ages 3–12) — Arousing interest in young students and increasing enthusiasm for learning. Supporting children's natural interests through Inquiry-Based Learning and a transdisciplinary approach.
Middle Years Programme (MYP) (ages 11–16) — Development of students' self-awareness, social responsibility and independent thinking skills. Through Interdisciplinary Learning and Personal Project, students develop abilities to connect different subjects.
Diploma Programme (DP) (ages 16–19) — Preparing students for university education and further life. Deep learning, research and social engagement through a comprehensive curriculum, Extended Essay, Theory of Knowledge (TOK) and Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS).
Career-related Programme (CP) (ages 16–19) — Preparing students for professional education and jobs. Develop professional skills and academic knowledge through Career-Related Study, reflective project and DP courses.
